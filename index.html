What are Carcinogens?
Carcinogens are substances capable of causing cancer by altering cellular DNA or promoting abnormal cell growth. Examples include tobacco smoke, asbestos fibers, benzene, formaldehyde, and ultraviolet radiation. Our focus at CarciCare is to identify and track these hazardous agents in various environments. By raising awareness about common carcinogens and their sources, we empower individuals to minimize exposure risks and make informed decisions for their health and well-being.
